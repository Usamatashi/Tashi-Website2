If you’re building accounting software aligned with **International Financial Reporting Standards Foundation (IFRS)**, focus on features that support accurate financial reporting, auditability, compliance, and multi-company operations.

Here’s a practical feature structure for a modern IFRS-based accounting system:

---

# Core Accounting Features

## 1. Chart of Accounts (IFRS Structured)

* Configurable account hierarchy
* Assets / Liabilities / Equity / Revenue / Expenses
* Parent-child ledger structure
* Multi-level account grouping
* IFRS-compliant account mapping

### Important

Allow:

* Custom account codes
* Segment-based accounts
* Department/project tagging

---

# General Ledger (GL)

## Must Have

* Double-entry accounting
* Journal vouchers
* Auto-balancing entries
* Recurring journals
* Reversing entries
* Posting lock periods
* Audit trail

## IFRS Requirements

* Immutable posting history
* User + timestamp tracking
* Adjustment entries
* Financial year closing

# Most Important IFRS Principles Your Software Must Respect

## 1. Accrual Accounting

Revenue/expense recognized when earned/incurred.

## 2. Double Entry Integrity

Every transaction balanced.

## 3. Auditability

Nothing silently editable.

## 4. Period Locking

Closed periods protected.

## 5. Traceability

Every number traceable to source transaction.
